Latest Investigations

Xarelto Linked to Serious Internal Bleeding Risk

Our attorneys have filed lawsuits against the makers of Xarelto alleging that the anticoagulant puts patients at risk for serious injuries because no antidote exists to reverse the drug’s blood-thinning effects. In addition to the absence of an antidote, patients also allege Xarelto’s makers marketed and sold the drug without properly notifying healthcare professionals and consumers of its risks.

Our attorneys are investigating allegations that Eliquis (apixaban), like Xarelto, has no reversal agent to stop the drug’s blood-thinning effects in the event of a bleeding emergency, such as a cerebral hemorrhage or kidney bleed.

Is Zofran to Blame for Birth Defects?

Our firm is investigating allegations that Zofran may cause birth defects in children. Specifically, it has been alleged that women who took Zofran—an anti-nausea drug that was neither tested nor approved for use in pregnant women—during their first trimester had a higher risk of delivering children with birth defects, including heart defects, craniofacial defects, and kidney malformations.

Health Insurer Premera Blue Cross Hit with Data Breach

Premera Blue Cross disclosed in mid-March that 11 million of the health insurance company’s customers may have had their personal and medical information stolen in a data breach. Though the hack reportedly took place in May 2014, the public and Premera’s customers were not notified until March 2015. Following the breach, Morgan & Morgan filed a class action lawsuit against Premera on behalf of those whose information was compromised. If you are a Premera member—or a member of Premera Blue Cross Blue Shield of Alaska, Vivacity Insurance or Connexion Insurance Solutions—we’d like to hear from you.

60 Minutes Investigation Leads to Litigation Against Lumber Liquidators

In March, our consumer protection attorneys filed lawsuits in Florida, Louisiana and California against Lumber Liquidators over its Chinese-made laminate flooring. Following a 60 Minutes investigation that tested several Lumber Liquidators products, it was revealed that nearly all of the tested samples contained high levels of formaldehyde, a chemical that has been linked to serious health problems in those exposed to it for prolonged periods of time. Formaldehyde is commonly used in the flooring industry as a binding agent; however, there are strict regulations as to how much of the chemical can be used. Among other allegations, our class action lawsuits allege the Chinese factories that supply Lumber Liquidators with the affected products purposely mislabeled them before shipping to meet formaldehyde regulations.

Shorter University Students’ Records Physically Stolen from Unlocked Room

Morgan & Morgan has filed a class action lawsuit against Rome, Georgia’s Shorter University on behalf of students whose personal and medical information was allegedly stolen from an unlocked filing cabinet in an unlocked room on campus in September 2014.

To be allowed to participate in school sporting activities, all student athletes were required to undergo physical examinations. The records of these physicals were kept in hard-copy format in a filing cabinet on campus. As many as 900 students’ records were physically taken out of this filing cabinet in an unlocked room by “an unauthorized individual,” who easily accessed the files because “the door was misaligned, and it was difficult to lock,” according to a report by the Rome News-Tribune.

Although the suit’s main allegation is that the school failed to adequately safeguard its students’ information, the class action also claims Shorter University failed to properly notify students that their data may have been stolen. After news of the theft came to light, Shorter University told only some students of the breach, while others claim they received no notice from the school, the suit alleges. Furthermore, according to news reports, as many as 30 Shorter students had difficulty filing tax returns due to the theft of their Social Security numbers and other information.
